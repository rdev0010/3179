Australia's population growth has experienced significant fluctuations over the years, influenced by major global events. The Global Financial Crisis in 2008 led to a temporary slowdown in growth, as economic challenges affected migration and other demographic factors. Similarly, the COVID-19 pandemic had a profound impact, causing a sharp decline in population growth due to border closures and decreased immigration. These events highlight the close relationship between global disruptions and population trends in Australia.
